Jim wants to know why the OmpA's with Ezβ redesigned surfaces won't insert. He has noticed that the redesigned ones have less aromatic residues than the natural protein. He wants to know why Ezβ derived frequencies are not reflecting the natural frequencies in OmpA.

 

Possibilities:

H1) Design method does not reproduce original frequencies.

- But if not reproducing the original frequencies is a problem, what's that mean for energy minimization as a design method? And the whole boltzmann distributution premise? Well it would mean that the non-randomness is important, cannot design randomly I guess.

 

H2) Frequencies of aromatics are lower in large than small proteins.

 

H3) Frequencies of aromatics are lower in oligomerization interfaces than in lipid interfaces

 

Testing H2

 

To test H2, I need a script that counts aromatics in the lipid-exposed surface of each protein and then divides that count by the total number of residues in the protein.

I will do this count under two conditions:

C1) Include all the residues in Ezβ 2.0: the residues in the structures as well as the residues in the homology models.

C2) Include all the residues in Ezβ original: only the residues in the structures.
